Wheel Alignment

Wheel alignment refers to adjusting the angles of your vehicle's wheels so that they are set to the manufacturer’s specifications. The alignment ensures that your tires make proper contact with the road, which is critical for safe and efficient driving. When your wheels are misaligned, it can cause uneven tire wear, poor handling, and reduced fuel efficiency.

Wheel Balancing

Wheel balancing is the process of ensuring that your vehicle’s wheels and tires rotate evenly at high speeds. Balancing is necessary to avoid vibration and ensure smooth operation of your vehicle, particularly when driving at higher speeds. When wheels are unbalanced, they can cause uneven wear on tires, vibrations in the steering wheel, and an uncomfortable driving experience.

Tyre Rotation

Tyre rotation is the process of moving your vehicle’s tyres from one position to another to ensure even tyre wear. Tyres on a vehicle wear differently depending on their position (front vs. rear) and whether your car is frontwheel, rear-wheel, or all-wheel drive. By rotating the tyres, you can ensure they wear more evenly, which extends their lifespan, improves performance, and enhances safety.

Oil Change

Oil change is one of the most essential maintenance services for any vehicle. Our oil service includes an oil filter and oil change. Regular oil changes keep your engine lubricated, reduce friction, and prevent overheating, ultimately extending the life of your engine.

Puncture repair

Puncture repair

A puncture repair typically involves removing the tire from the vehicle, identifying the location and size of the puncture, and cleaning the affected area. A patch or plug is then used to seal the hole securely, restoring the tire's integrity. Puncture repairs are usually effective for small, non-sidewall punctures, but larger or sidewall damages may require tire replacement. Timely repairs can extend the life of a tire and ensure the safety and performance of the vehicle.

Tyre Change

Tyres are one of the most critical components of your vehicle. They provide the necessary contact with the road, supporting the weight of your car and ensuring a smooth and safe ride. However, like all parts of a vehicle, tyres wear out over time and eventually need to be replaced. A tyre change is a simple yet crucial maintenance task that helps keep your vehicle running efficiently, safely, and reliably.

Tyre rotation should be done regularly, and most vehicle manufacturers recommend rotating the tires every 6,000 to 8,000 miles (or about every other oil change).
